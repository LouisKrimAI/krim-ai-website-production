Krim-Nyāya
The validation gate that makes AI provable.

Krim-Nyāya is a pre-execution logic gate drawn from classical Navya-Nyāya epistemology, the Indian tradition of rigorous inference. Every agent decision passes through it before acting.

The gate asks: Is the evidence valid? Is the reasoning sound? Is the action proportionate? 33 validators across three families answer before the system acts.

Pramāṇa
Evidence validity

Checks whether the knowledge the agent is acting on is well-founded: data quality, source reliability, consistency of inputs.

  • Data completeness and integrity
  • Source attribution and reliability
  • Input consistency across channels
Doṣa
Error detection

Checks whether the reasoning contains known failure modes: contradictions, hallucinations, out-of-distribution predictions.

  • Logical contradiction detection
  • Confidence threshold enforcement
  • Distribution boundary checks
Yogyatā
Fitness & proportion

Checks whether the proposed action is appropriate given the context: policy alignment, regulatory scope, proportionality to the decision stakes.

  • Policy and regulatory alignment
  • Action proportionality
  • Contextual appropriateness
Pass
All checks satisfied. System acts. Full audit log written at execution.
Amber
Partial confidence. System flags for human review before proceeding.
Fail
Check not satisfied. Action blocked. Reason documented. Human notified.
A hallucinated action never reaches your borrower.
