Chief Risk Officer

AI is making decisions your name is on.

And the reasoning is a black box.

The AI systems your institution has adopted are fast, sometimes confidently wrong, and unable to explain themselves. In a regulated lending environment that is not acceptable: when a regulator asks why a decision was made, ‘the model said so’ is not an answer. When a loan goes wrong, that question lands on you.

CRO
What KrimOS delivers
  • Every AI decision has a verdict and a reason before it acts. Krim-Nyāya gates every agent action: pass, amber, or fail, with a traceable logic chain that exists before anything executes.
  • Risk signals surfaced before they become arrears. Kovida monitors behavioural patterns across the performing book. Deteriorating accounts are flagged before the payment is missed, while there is still time to act.
  • A model that improves with your own portfolio data. Kovida learns from every transaction your institution processes, making risk calls sharper over time for your specific book.
What changes
  • Any regulator request for a decision trail is answered from the record, because the record was written before the decision acted.
  • Early-warning signals live across the performing book. Deterioration surfaces before the roll.
  • Risk calls sharpen with every repayment cycle as Kovida tunes to your borrower base.
The shift: From AI that acts without explanation to AI that validates before it acts, with a complete, time-stamped audit trail built for inspection. You stay accountable. We make it defensible.

Head of Collections

Volume is rising.
Contact rates are falling.

Strategy is reactive because the data arrives too late.

Your team is working harder on a portfolio that keeps growing. But the tools haven't kept up. Dialler lists built on lagging data. Scripts that don't adapt to the borrower. Strategies that chase arrears after they have already formed.

The accounts that matter most are buried in a queue ordered by account age, blind to recovery probability.

Collections
What KrimOS delivers
  • Dynamic prioritisation built on recovery probability. The outreach co-worker surfaces the contacts most likely to resolve, updated continuously as Kovida refines its read of each borrower's behaviour.
  • Outreach that adapts to the borrower. Contact strategy, channel, timing, and resolution options are matched to each account's profile and history.
  • Resolution offers presented at the moment the borrower is most receptive. The system monitors payment-intent signals and triggers the right offer before the account deteriorates further: payment plan, settlement, or hardship.
What changes
  • Routine outreach runs on its own. Collector capacity concentrates on complex cases and relationship-sensitive accounts.
  • Outreach reaches the right borrower at the right time, through the channel they actually answer.
  • Resolution happens earlier, while more options are still open. Escalation becomes the exception path.
The shift: From reactive, volume-driven collections to a model where the system surfaces the right account, at the right time, with the right approach, while your team focuses where human judgement creates the most value.

Head of Servicing

Borrowers expect digital-first.

Your servicing model was built for branches.

The gap between what borrowers expect and what your servicing infrastructure can deliver is widening. CSAT scores track it. Call centre volumes confirm it. Every routine query that reaches the branch is a process failure: an EMI balance, a statement, a prepayment request.

And reconciliation sits underneath it all: payment matching done manually, exceptions buried in spreadsheets, errors discovered weeks after they occur.

Servicing
What KrimOS delivers
  • Digital-first self-serve for the full servicing range. EMI queries, statements, hardship applications, and prepayment requests, handled digitally, without branch intervention, in the borrower's preferred language and channel.
  • Automated reconciliation: exceptions surface the moment they occur. Payment matching runs automatically, and anything outside tolerance is flagged the day it happens, while the cost of correction is still low.
  • Hardship flagged before accounts deteriorate. Kovida identifies borrowers showing early servicing stress, prompting proactive outreach before a missed payment becomes an arrear.
What changes
  • Digital channels absorb the routine query load. Branch and call-centre time goes to the moments that need a person.
  • Reconciliation exceptions surface in real time: a cleaner book and cleaner reporting.
  • The servicing team moves from answering queries to managing relationships.
The shift: From a servicing model built around branch capacity to one where digital channels handle routine volume, and your team focuses on the servicing moments that require human judgement and relationship management.
