On 24 June 2026 the Reserve Bank of India published its draft Model Risk Management guidance. One clause describes, almost word for word, the mechanism KrimOS is built around.

“…mechanisms to verify and corroborate model outputs prior to their use…”

Para 54(1)(ii)  ·  RBI draft Model Risk Management guidance  ·  24 June 2026
The mechanism

A pre-execution validation gate is exactly that mechanism: it verifies every output before the system acts on it. That is how KrimOS is built: every decision an agent proposes passes through Krim-Nyāya before the system acts on it.

The verdict

Krim-Nyāya returns a pass, amber or fail verdict on every decision, with the reason recorded before any action executes. A decision that fails validation never reaches a customer, a core system or a ledger.

The trail

Every gated decision leaves a time-stamped record of what was proposed, what was checked and why the verdict landed as it did. The trail is built for inspection from the first transaction onwards.

What the draft requires, and where KrimOS answers.

Eight requirements from the draft, read against the architecture. Each requirement maps to a mechanism that is already part of how the system works.

The draft says What it requires Where KrimOS answers
¶ 54(1)(ii) Verify and corroborate model outputs prior to their use. Krim-Nyāya, the pre-execution validation gate. Every decision is checked before the system acts on it.
¶ 60(ii) Override and kill-switch on model-driven actions. The gate is prospective, so a failing action is blocked before it commits. Operators can step in and halt any co-worker from the command centre.
¶ 54(2) Hallucination control in generative outputs. Customer answers grounded in the record.
¶ 54(6) Manage output variability through confidence measures. Calibrated validator tiers score every decision.
¶ 60(i) Human-in-command oversight of model operations (HITL). Krim-Govern enforces the policy; Kupa, the command centre, gives your teams the controls to see, set and step in.
¶ 57 / 23 Traceability of model decisions, with 10-year retention. Krim-Ledger, an immutable record of every gated action.
¶ 48 Audit rights and exit from vendor models. Sovereign local deployment. The system runs in your infrastructure, in your jurisdiction.
¶ 8 / 29 The regulated entity remains accountable; validation must be independent. Krim generates the validation evidence. Accountability stays with your institution, and the record gives you the means to exercise it.
