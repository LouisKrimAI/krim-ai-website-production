## 2 · The problem
EYEBROW: WHY LENDING STALLS
H2: Two teams, one wall.
COPY: Lending is run by two workforces — the people who speak to customers, and the people who keep the books — held apart by tickets, spreadsheets and hand-offs. Together they're 40–60% of what every loan costs. AI could close the gap, but a regulated action you can't explain is a risk no lender will take. So the work stays manual.
PRESENTATION: two tall glass columns — front office / back office — split by one luminous seam (the wall); the cost figure large in the gap; the seam glows faintly, foreshadowing its dissolve. Architectural, restrained.

## 3 · The lifecycle  (CENTREPIECE)
EYEBROW: END TO END
H2: Two threads. One journey.
COPY: The same system carries both sides of the wall through the whole loan — Kira with the customer, Karta in the back office — meeting at every step, every action validated as it passes.
PRESENTATION: a horizontal JOURNEY SPINE from Application to Payoff, six luminous stage-nodes. Two translucent threads travel in parallel (Kira above, Karta below), converging at each node where a cyan→mint pulse marks the action clearing validation. Scroll advances the journey; the active stage rises as a glass card; the spine fills mint as the loan completes. Mobile: spine vertical, threads either side. This is the page's signature motion — no table.
STAGES (customer line · back-office line):
- Onboarding — greets, qualifies, guides · scores the lead, runs KYC, reads the documents
- Decision — gathers what's missing, sets expectations · assembles the credit picture, checks policy, preps the sanction  [caption: Krim builds the picture — the credit decision stays with you.]
- Disbursal — walks the borrower through signing · drafts the agreement, clears compliance, releases funds
- Servicing — payments, statements, questions, always on · maintains accounts, reconciles, monitors
- Collections — reminders and plans, hardship handled gently · segments risk, flags early, escalates
- Closure — payoff, the NOC, the next conversation · settles, reports, feeds the learning back
