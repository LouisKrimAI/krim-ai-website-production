## 2 · Formalising judgment
EYEBROW: FORMAL LOGIC
H2: Turning a 2,000-year-old logic into runnable checks.
COPY: Navya-Nyāya gave the world a precise account of what makes an inference sound. Our work is to make that precision executable — to translate its tests for grounded premises, classes of error and fitness-for-action into thirty-three validators a runtime can apply to every action, in milliseconds, before it happens.
PRESENTATION: a quiet "translation" motif — classical logic becoming a computable validator.

## 3 · A world model of operations
EYEBROW: THE FRONTIER
H2: Learning how an operation truly behaves.
COPY: When one runtime observes an entire operation — every action, outcome and reason on one record — it becomes possible to model how that operation actually works, not how a process diagram says it should. Building that model, and learning from it without ever pooling a customer's data, is an open frontier. It's the one we're working on.
PRESENTATION: the world-model field, framed as a research object, not a product feature.

## 4 · The discipline of pre-execution
EYEBROW: VALIDATION SCIENCE
H2: Validating before, not auditing after.
COPY: Most of AI safety is reactive — measure, monitor, catch. We work on the harder, quieter discipline: deciding, with formal rigour and at runtime speed, whether an action should be allowed to happen at all. Getting that right is what lets autonomy and accountability coexist.
PRESENTATION: restrained.
