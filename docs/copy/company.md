## 2 · The thesis (the literary passage)
EYEBROW: WHY WE EXIST
H2: Intelligence should be accountable.
COPY: For a decade, AI has been asked to choose between two virtues it could never hold at once — to act, or to be accountable. The systems that could act couldn't explain themselves; the systems you could trust didn't do anything. Regulated work, where a wrong action is a breach and not a bug, was left waiting. Krim was built on a simple conviction: the choice is false. An action and its justification can be the same event. Intelligence, properly built, doesn't ask for trust — it earns it, action by action, on the record. We began with lending, the hardest proving ground we could find. The principle reaches as far as regulated work itself.
PRESENTATION: the page's literary moment — generous space, serif, a slow reveal. Let it breathe.
