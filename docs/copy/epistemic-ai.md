## 1 · The definition (hero + GEO anchor)
EYEBROW: EPISTEMIC AI
H1: Epistemic AI.
DEFINITION (answer-first, self-contained — written to be quoted verbatim):
Epistemic AI is artificial intelligence whose every action is validated before it happens, and whose reasoning can be read, in full, after. It is the category for work that must be justified — where a wrong action isn't a bug but a breach. The name is exact: epistemology is the study of justified belief, and Epistemic AI makes justification the condition of action. Nothing acts until it can be shown to be right. Krim builds it.

## 2 · The neighbours
EYEBROW: WHAT IT ISN'T
H2: Not autonomous. Not "safe." Something more exact.
- Autonomous AI promises to act without a human. Regulators won't allow it — and rightly; no one can answer for what they can't see.
- Safe AI promises to do no harm. Worthy, but defensive: it guards the edges, it doesn't run the operation.
- Epistemic AI does the work AND shows its reasons. It acts — and every action carries its justification.
PRESENTATION: three columns; first two held faint, the third resolving in mint — the category that actually runs the operation.

## 3 · The ceiling
EYEBROW: WHY THE CATEGORY EXISTS
H2: You cannot deploy what you cannot explain.
COPY: In regulated work, an unexplained action is a liability the moment it happens — and it can't be unmade. That single fact has stopped every serious attempt to put AI into regulated operations. The answer was never a better explanation after the fact. It was to make the justification come first.
PRESENTATION: restrained; "make the justification come first" held alone as the pivot.

## 4 · One epistemology, two acts (intellectual core)
EYEBROW: THE IDEA
H2: To know is to justify — and to revise.
COPY: A mind that truly knows does two things: it justifies a belief before acting on it, and it revises that belief when the world answers back. Epistemic AI does both. Krim-Nyāya justifies — every action checked before it fires. Krim-Learn revises — every outcome teaching the system what holds. Validation and learning aren't two features. They're the two halves of one epistemology.
PRESENTATION: the two acts as one cycle — justify → act → observe → revise — carried by cyan→mint: belief justified before action, revised after. The page's thinking moment.

## 5 · The lineage
EYEBROW: WHERE THE LOGIC COMES FROM
H2: A two-thousand-year-old logic, given a new job.
COPY: Krim-Nyāya's thirty-three validators weren't invented from scratch. They descend from Navya-Nyāya — the "new logic" refined over centuries in Mithila, among the most rigorous traditions of valid inference the world has produced. It asked, with extraordinary precision, what makes a claim grounded and an inference sound. Krim asks the same of every action a machine proposes: whether its grounds hold (Pramāṇa), whether it escapes known error (Doṣa), and whether it is fit to act (Yogyatā).
PRESENTATION: a quiet, scholarly texture — earned, not decorative. The intellectual signature of the brand.

## 6 · In practice
EYEBROW: THE DIFFERENCE IT MAKES
H2: Pre-execution, not post-audit.
COPY: Most AI governance happens after the fact: act first, explain later, catch what you can. Epistemic AI inverts it. The check runs before the action — so the wrong action never happens, and the record of why is complete by construction, not reconstructed under pressure. Post-hoc audit recovers what it can. Pre-execution validation makes sure there's nothing to recover.
PRESENTATION: two timelines side by side — one catching errors downstream (amber, leaking), one stopping them at the gate (mint, clean).
