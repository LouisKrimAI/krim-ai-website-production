## 2 · The loop
EYEBROW: THE LOOP
H2: One path for every action.
BODY: Perceive. Reason. Plan. Validate. Act. Every co-worker, every task, runs the same five steps — and the fourth is the one the others serve. Validation isn't a review after the work; it's the gate the work must pass to become real.
PRESENTATION: the five steps as an elegant flow; Validate is a luminous gate the others lead into. An action travels the path and clears the gate — cyan resolving to mint. The page's quiet centrepiece motion.

## 3 · Power one — Judgment
EYEBROW: POWER ONE · JUDGMENT
H2: Krim-Nyāya: the gate.
BODY: Before anything fires, it goes to Krim-Nyāya — thirty-three validators that ask, in formal logic, whether this action is allowed to happen at all. They work in three families:
[CARD] Pramāṇa — is it grounded? Every premise the action rests on has to hold. Unverifiable, it doesn't proceed.
[CARD] Doṣa — is it sound? The action is checked against every known way this kind of thing goes wrong.
[CARD] Yogyatā — is it fit? Right moment, right person, right channel, right purpose — wrong on any, and it waits.
BODY: Most actions clear. Some don't — and that's the point. A blocked action doesn't vanish; it's flagged amber and handed to a person, with the rule that stopped it and the reason in plain words. A system you can trust is one you can watch refuse.
[CARD, large]: 33 validators. Nothing executes unvalidated.
FOOTNOTE: The logic is Navya-Nyāya — a formal tradition of valid inference refined over centuries in Mithila — applied now to machine action. The full story lives on Epistemic AI.
PRESENTATION: signature device — an action approaches the gate and is checked by the three families in turn; pass glows mint, amber peels off to an "exception" lane. Honest and legible; you can watch it refuse.

## 4 · Power two — Intelligence
EYEBROW: POWER TWO · INTELLIGENCE
H2: Krim-Learn: the mind.
BODY: Validation makes the system safe to run. Krim-Learn makes it worth running twice. Because every action and its outcome land on one record, Krim-Learn can see what actually worked — and feed it back. Ten learning loops turn a day of operations into a sharper next day.
BODY: Over time this becomes something no point tool can build: a connected model of how the whole operation behaves — what works, what doesn't, and where things stall and why. (In lending, say: which sequence brings a late account back to good standing.) The longer it runs, the more it knows.
BODY: It's disciplined, not magic. A pattern only joins the shared library once it clears an effectiveness threshold; what's shared is anonymised and aggregated; any institution can opt out. The improvement comes from the runtime itself — not from more engineering.
[CARD — the curve]: Quarter one, a baseline. Quarter two, measurably better. By year two, materially beyond where it began.
PRESENTATION: a quiet "world-model" field that visibly enriches as actions feed it — points connecting, brightening — paired with the rising curve. Restraint over spectacle; it should feel like accrual, not fireworks.

## 5 · The rest of the runtime
EYEBROW: THE RUNTIME, IN FULL
H2: Five quieter modules hold it together.
BODY: Nyāya and Learn get the attention. These make them possible:
[CARD] Krim-Core — orchestration: routes each task, runs the workflows, handles retries and hand-offs.
[CARD] Krim-Fabric — knowledge: the rules of each jurisdiction, each institution's policies, the shared pattern library.
[CARD] Krim-Govern — policy: a seven-level hierarchy of law, regulation and house rules, enforced per tenant.
[CARD] Krim-Ledger — the record: every action logged, sealed, and metered (below).
[CARD] Krim-Sense — telemetry: the metrics, alerts and dashboards that show what's happening.
PRESENTATION: five quiet glass chips arranged as a support layer around the two powers — clearly secondary, clearly holding them up. Not a flat list; a constellation.

## 6 · The ledger
EYEBROW: ONE RECORD
H2: Three jobs, one record.
BODY: Krim-Ledger writes down everything — every action, decision and validation — to an immutable, sealed trail. From that single source, it does three jobs at once:
[CARD] Evidence. The audit trail a regulator can replay. Inspection responses in minutes, not days.
[CARD] Intelligence. The same record is what Krim-Learn learns from. Proof and improvement, one source.
[CARD] Meter. Every action is measured in Krim Work Units — your own clear view of what the operation costs.
[CARD, large]: Compliance, intelligence and cost — from one immutable record.
PRESENTATION: signature device — one sealed record at the centre, three streams flowing out of it (evidence · intelligence · meter). The elegance is the single source; show that, not three separate widgets.
