# DIFFERENTIATION.md

The site's differentiation argument is structural, not feature-based. KrimOS is presented as the only architecture that makes regulated AI actions provable before they happen — not as a better version of something that already exists, but as the answer to a structural gap the existing category leaves open. The site is careful not to name competitors and careful not to claim certifications that are not yet held; its differentiation claims are based on architecture, not on market comparisons.

---

## Architectural differentiation

The site's clearest architectural claim is the negative claim: no other architecture produces the substrate KrimOS produces. From krim-content.md and surfaced on /research/world-lending-model and /platform/kendra: "One ledger recording every action, its choice set, its reasoning, and its outcome across the whole lifecycle — behind a deny-by-default pre-execution gate over validated Kriya primitives. Incumbent ledgers record transactions, not reasoning or rejected alternatives; point tools see one slice."

The platform page states: "That is why one operating system beats a drawer of point tools — and why it gets better the longer it runs." (/platform, "Why it holds together" section)

The company page describes the architectural thesis: "Krim made proof the runtime — every action validated before it can execute — and kept the whole system inside the perimeter, where the data already lives." (/company)

The architecture page's one-paragraph summary in KRIMOS-ARCHITECTURE-LENDING.md (the internal spine): "KrimOS is a single governed runtime for the lending lifecycle, built so two things are true at once: a lender's AI is provable before it acts, and the runtime lays down the substrate a model of the lending operation could one day be built on." The site treats this as an external claim grounded in architecture, not in deployment track record.

## Process differentiation: pre-execution vs. post-audit framing

This is the site's sharpest and most repeated differentiation claim. It appears across multiple pages as a direct contrast:

- "Validated, not audited after the fact" (lending page eyebrow)
- "Pre-execution, not post-audit. The proof is recorded as the work runs." (trust page GlassCard)
- "Validation moves in front of the action and the evidence is complete by construction — so audits and inspections are answered from a record that is already there." (/platform, "Compliance becomes built-in, not bolted-on")
- "It is not a wrapper, a policy file or a downstream check — it is the runtime itself." (krim-content.md, describing Krim-Nyāya)
- "Compliance violations are not reduced; they are made structurally impossible." (krim-content.md)
- "Wrong actions never reach a borrower." (homepage, power 1 tagline)

The government page's middle card — given accent treatment as "the heart" — states: "Before any co-worker acts, the proposed action is checked against the rules that govern it. Nothing executes without a basis recorded — compliance becomes a property of the runtime, not a department's vigilance." (/government)

The lending page's problem framing: "AI could close the gap, but a regulated action you can't explain is a risk no lender will take. So the work stays manual." The resolution: "KrimOS gates every action before it fires — so the work you couldn't trust to software becomes work you can." (/lending)

## Sovereignty and data residency positioning

Sovereignty is described as a structural requirement, not a deployment option. The phrasing is consistent and deliberate across the site:

- "Sovereignty is a commitment, not a deployment option." (trust page sub-line; company page; krim-content.md designated tagline)
- "Sovereign by construction. Auditable by default." (trust page h1; homepage close)
- "No foreign API anywhere in the loop." (lending deployment section; trust hero)
- "Customer data, model weights, orchestration and telemetry stay inside the institution's perimeter." (trust hero; company thesis)

The trust page makes the case explicitly: sovereign on-prem is "Default · regulated lenders & government. The full stack runs inside your data centre. Data, model weights, orchestration and telemetry stay behind your perimeter — nothing leaves." The government page's first "Why Krim fits" card: "KrimOS runs inside your perimeter, in your jurisdiction. Model weights, orchestration and citizens' data never leave — the property that rules most AI out is the one Krim is built on."

The site does not name providers being excluded, but krim-content.md notes the market context: "Third-party concentration: top-3 cloud ≈ 73% of FS cloud, ≈ 44% of model provision, ≈ 33% of data services — the empirical case for sovereignty."

## Full-lifecycle vs. point-tool positioning

The platform page names the gap: "That is why one operating system beats a drawer of point tools." The homepage's challenge section: "A dozen siloed tools, none of them seeing the whole operation — and your data can't even leave the bank."

The lending page describes the two-sided operation: "Kira meets the customer on every channel; Karta co-workers do the back-office work. They meet at every stage — and each action passes the validation gate before it executes." Section eyebrow: "End to end / Both sides of the wall, on one system."

The enterprise page: "KrimOS sits over the systems you already run, reading from each and writing back on validated channels. One way of working across all of them — nothing torn out."

The site explicitly frames the lifecycle coverage: sourcing and onboarding, underwriting and decision, disbursal, servicing, collections and hardship, closure and re-engagement — all six stages, both customer-facing (Kira) and back-office (Karta).

## "Only" and "first" claims the site makes

The site is disciplined about not making unverifiable superlatives. The near-"only" claims that are grounded in architecture:

- "The negative architectural claim is true today and needs no deployment: incumbent ledgers record transactions, not reasoning or rejected alternatives; point tools see one slice." (POSITIONING.md, KRIMOS-ARCHITECTURE-LENDING.md — internal framing, but referenced on the platform and research pages)
- The research page: "Nothing acts until it has cleared the model" — framing the World Lending Model's eventual uniqueness
- The company thesis: "Krim made proof the runtime" — framing the category origination claim
- krim-content.md: "The category that wins regulated lending validates every action before it fires and exposes reasoning an auditor can read end to end." (epistemic AI section)

The site avoids the word "only" and "first" explicitly, preferring architectural statements over superlative claims.

## What the site says competitors and the status quo cannot do

The site frames the gap through the challenge rather than naming competitors. From the homepage:

- "AI gets things wrong. And in lending, a wrong answer to a borrower is a violation — not a typo."
- "AI only advises. Recommendations don't clear the backlog. The work, and the cost, stay with your team."
- "No unified intelligence. A dozen siloed tools, none of them seeing the whole operation — and your data can't even leave the bank."

From krim-content.md's "Why generic AI fails here":
- "General-purpose AI hallucinates; post-hoc audit cannot recover what pre-execution validation prevents."
- "Any AI requiring data egress is the wrong choice" (for regulated jurisdictions)
- "Bolting it onto each system, one at a time, never ends." (/enterprise)

From POSITIONING.md: "The entire industry is racing to make banking agentic; nobody has made it provable; and in a regulated bank, unprovable autonomy is unshippable. Krim owns provable."

The lending page's problem framing is the clearest statement: "A regulated action you can't explain is a risk no lender will take. So the work stays manual." The differentiation claim is that KrimOS is the only architecture that removes this blocker at the structural level.
