# CAPABILITY-WEIGHTING.md

The site foregrounds pre-execution validation most heavily, as the claim that unlocks deployment; the agent harness second, as the workforce that delivers operational value; and the World Lending Model third, as the intelligence direction that compounds on top of the other two. This ordering is visible across hero copy, section count, the depth of technical treatment, and where conversion CTAs are placed. The report below is purely observational — it describes what the site does, not what it should do.

---

## The three pillars and their site weight

**Pillar A: Agent harness for full lending operations**
**Pillar B: Pre-execution validation / policy runtime**
**Pillar C: World model for lending intelligence**

---

## Hero copy — which pillar leads

The homepage hero (HomeHero component) is not fully visible in the page.tsx source, but the OG meta description gives the content hierarchy: "a world model for lending with AI co-workers that run the whole lifecycle, every action validated before it acts, inside your own walls." The order is: world model first, co-workers second, validation third — but in the homepage sections themselves the first substantive content section is titled "The challenge" and its three problems are: AI gets things wrong (validation problem), AI only advises (agent harness problem), no unified intelligence (world model problem).

The "Three powers" section (section 3 on the homepage, with the heading "The three answers") lists them in this order:
1. Pre-execution validation — "Wrong actions never reach a borrower."
2. AI co-workers — "The work gets done, not just recommended."
3. The World Lending Model — "One intelligence across your whole operation."

The /platform hero headline is "One operating system for regulated operations" with the sub-line "AI co-workers do the work; every action is validated before it executes, and the system gets sharper from every outcome it records." Validation and the agent harness share the sub-line equally; the World Lending Model appears as "sharper from every outcome."

**Conclusion on hero copy:** Pillar B (validation) and Pillar A (agent harness) share the hero position; the World Lending Model is present but subordinate in hero copy.

- **On-brand lines used as callouts repeatedly:** "Validated before it acts," "Pre-execution, not post-audit," "Compliance as physics" — all reference Pillar B. These appear on the homepage, lending, platform, and kendra pages.

### Pillar A (Agent harness) — dedicated pages and sections

- **/platform/kriya:** Full page for the vocabulary underlying the harness.

### Pillar C (World Lending Model) — dedicated pages and sections

- **/research/world-lending-model:** Sole dedicated page — six sections, full treatment of the component models, agent harness, stack coverage.

**Pillar C has one dedicated page; Pillars A and B each have two or more dedicated layer pages plus representation across all domain pages.**

## CTA placement — which pillar drives conversion

Primary CTAs on every page point to /contact (Book a demo). The secondary CTAs on key pages reveal emphasis:

- Homepage: secondary CTA in the platform section points to /platform (neutral); secondary CTA in the world model section points to /research/world-lending-model (Pillar C); secondary CTA in the close points to /contact.
- /platform: primary CTA "Book a demo," secondary CTA "See how it validates" → /platform/kendra (Pillar B).
- /lending: secondary CTA in hero "See how KrimOS works" → /platform (neutral).
- /platform/kendra (the validation page): no secondary CTA; close is a single GlassCard with no outbound link.
- /research/world-lending-model: secondary CTA "See the runtime it learns from" → /platform/kendra (Pillar B).

The notable finding: the one secondary CTA on the main /platform page directs to Kendra with the label "See how it validates" — the site's most prominent secondary CTA, on the highest-traffic platform page, points to the validation story. The World Lending Model is a prominent secondary CTA from the homepage but is not used to drive conversion from the domain pages (lending, enterprise, government, msme all CTA to /contact only).

**CTA conclusion:** Pillar B (validation) is the site's preferred conversion mechanism — "See how it validates" is the single most prominent secondary CTA. Pillar C is surfaced prominently from the homepage but not used as a conversion driver from domain pages.
